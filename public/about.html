About

Practical Japan guidance for remote workers and digital nomads.

Japan Remote Guide helps international remote workers understand what it actually takes to work, stay, move, and plan safely in Japan for a temporary stay.

Run from Japan, with local context

Japan Remote Guide is operated by a Japan-born, Japan-based independent publisher. The goal is to translate local context, official information, and everyday practical friction into clearer decisions for visitors.

Mission

Explain visa checks, city tradeoffs, weather risks, and local setup steps clearly

Japan is easy to admire from the outside, but working from Japan requires more than a list of places to visit. This site focuses on decisions that affect daily work: visa fit, city choice, weather risk, accommodation friction, workspace reliability, transport, language barriers, and local etiquette.

Editorial standard

Practical, cautious, and source-aware

How guidance is written

  • Official sources are prioritized for visa, safety, weather, and public information.
  • Local context is used to explain practical friction that may not be obvious before arrival.
  • Advice is framed as planning guidance, not as a guarantee that one route will fit every visitor.

Important limits

  • This site is not an immigration law firm, tax advisor, insurance advisor, or travel agency.
  • Visa, tax, insurance, employment, and legal decisions should be confirmed with official sources or qualified professionals.
  • Rules and local conditions can change, so time-sensitive pages should be checked before use.
