About Japan Remote Guide

A clearer way to plan remote work in Japan.

Japan Remote Guide is an independent, Tokyo-based resource that organizes practical information for international remote workers preparing for temporary stays in Japan.

Operated by

Dommy

Sole Proprietor (Japan), established July 2026 in Tokyo.

Mission

Make living and working remotely in Japan easier to understand.

Japan Remote Guide exists to organize practical, reliable information about staying, working, moving around, and preparing for daily life in Japan—so remote workers can make calmer decisions before they arrive.

YI

About the Founder

Yuusuke Iyadomi

Japan Remote Guide is founded by Yuusuke Iyadomi and operated through Dommy, a sole proprietorship based in Tokyo, Japan. The site is built from a local perspective with attention to the practical questions international remote workers often need to verify before choosing Japan as a temporary base.

The editorial approach is intentionally measured: clarify what can be explained, point readers toward official sources, and avoid presenting complex immigration, tax, insurance, or legal topics as one-size-fits-all advice.

Editorial Policy

Careful research, cautious wording, regular review.

Source-first research

Information is researched carefully using official and authoritative sources whenever possible, including the Japanese Government, Immigration Services Agency, Ministry of Foreign Affairs, JNTO, local governments, and other relevant organizations.

Practical interpretation

Articles are written to help readers understand real planning decisions, while distinguishing general guidance from topics that should be confirmed with official bodies or qualified professionals.

Updates when rules change

Japan Remote Guide does not claim perfect accuracy. Regulations, local procedures, and travel conditions can change, so articles are reviewed and updated when meaningful changes are identified.
