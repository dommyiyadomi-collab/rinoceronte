# Source Record: Japan Tax System for Digital Nomads and Temporary Remote Workers

- Reference date: 2026-07-19
- Intended use: source notes for a future website explainer on taxes for digital nomads and temporary remote workers in Japan.
- Scope: Japanese individual income tax, tax residence categories, non-permanent resident/non-resident scope, Japan-source income, treaty/183-day issues, filing/withholding basics, local inhabitant tax timing, consumption tax flags, and social insurance/pension caveats.
- Caveat: This is source research, not tax or immigration advice. The National Tax Agency (NTA) tells taxpayers to calculate and file according to their own facts, and treaty provisions differ by treaty, so individual cases should be checked with the relevant tax office or a licensed tax professional. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf), [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm)

## Executive Summary

Japan's Digital Nomad status of residence is an immigration status for remote work in Japan for a period not exceeding six months, with no extension granted; it requires evidence including annual income of at least JPY 10 million and insurance covering death, injury, or illness during the stay with medical-treatment compensation of at least JPY 10 million. [MOFA Digital Nomad visa page](https://www.mofa.go.jp/ca/fna/pagewe_000001_00046.html), [ISA Digital Nomad status page](https://www.moj.go.jp/isa/applications/status/designatedactivities53_00001.html?lang=en)

Tax residence is determined separately from the visa label: for Japanese income tax, an individual is a resident if they have a "JUSHO (domicile)" in Japan or have had a "KYOSHO (residence)" continuously for one year or more; an individual who is not a resident is a non-resident. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf), [NTA consumption tax basic definitions quoting Income Tax Act resident/non-resident definitions](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/consumption_tax/01.htm)

A non-Japanese resident who has had a domicile or residence in Japan for an aggregate period of five years or less within the preceding ten years is a non-permanent resident (NPR). [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf)

Residents other than NPRs are taxed on whole income, NPRs are taxed on income other than foreign-source income plus foreign-source income paid in Japan or remitted to Japan, and non-residents are taxed on domestic-source income only. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf)

For remote workers, the physical work location is a key Japan tax fact: NTA materials and Japan's Income Tax Act framework treat salary, wages, bonuses, and compensation for employment or other personal services performed in Japan as domestic-source income, even where payment is made outside Japan. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf), [NTA No.2878 domestic-source income](https://www.nta.go.jp/taxes/shiraberu/taxanswer/gensen/2878.htm), [Income Tax Act translation, Japanese Law Translation](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/52/en)

The "183-day" concept is mainly a tax treaty issue, not Japan's basic domestic residence test; the NTA says a non-resident may need to consider the applicable treaty where they stay in Japan for 183 days or less and earned income is not paid by an employer in Japan and is not borne by an employer's permanent establishment (PE) in Japan. [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm), [MOF list of Japan's tax conventions](https://www.mof.go.jp/english/policy/tax_policy/tax_conventions/tax_convetion_list_en.html)

Digital Nomad status allowing six months in Japan does not by itself guarantee a tax treaty exemption; treaty relief depends on the exact treaty, treaty residence, day-count wording, employer/payment facts, PE or fixed-base facts, and timely procedures where Japanese withholding is involved. [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm), [NTA treaty application forms](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/withholing/tax_convention.htm), [MOF list of Japan's tax conventions](https://www.mof.go.jp/english/policy/tax_policy/tax_conventions/tax_convetion_list_en.html)

## Digital Nomad Status of Residence: Tax-Relevant Immigration Facts

MOFA describes the Digital Nomad visa as a Specified Visa: Designated Activities status for individuals wishing to work remotely in Japan for a period not exceeding six months, and states the period of stay is six months with no extension granted. [MOFA Digital Nomad visa page](https://www.mofa.go.jp/ca/fna/pagewe_000001_00046.html)

MOFA lists required documents for the Digital Nomad applicant, including planned activities/period of stay, evidence that annual income is JPY 10 million or more, and evidence of insurance covering death, injury, or illness during the stay with medical treatment compensation of JPY 10 million or more. [MOFA Digital Nomad visa page](https://www.mofa.go.jp/ca/fna/pagewe_000001_00046.html)

The Immigration Services Agency (ISA) Digital Nomad materials identify this as Designated Activities for digital nomads, state that required materials are for stays not exceeding six months, and provide a dedicated Q&A. [ISA Digital Nomad status page](https://www.moj.go.jp/isa/applications/status/designatedactivities53_00001.html?lang=en), [ISA Digital Nomad Q&A PDF](https://www.moj.go.jp/isa/content/001422249.pdf)

ISA's public Digital Nomad outline says the status of residence is "Designated Activities" for six months, is not renewable, and is outside residence-card issuance. [ISA Digital Nomad outline PDF](https://www.moj.go.jp/isa/content/001417262.pdf), [ISA Japanese Digital Nomad status page](https://www.moj.go.jp/isa/applications/status/designatedactivities10_00001.html)

Inference: because tax residence is determined under tax rules and Digital Nomad status is a non-renewable immigration category for a stay not exceeding six months, many Digital Nomad entrants will be non-residents for Japanese income tax, but that is not automatic if the facts show a Japanese "JUSHO (domicile)" or a continuously held "KYOSHO (residence)" for one year or more. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf), [MOFA Digital Nomad visa page](https://www.mofa.go.jp/ca/fna/pagewe_000001_00046.html)

## Tax Residency Categories

For Japanese income tax, a resident is an individual who has a "JUSHO (domicile)" in Japan or owns a "KYOSHO (residence)" continuously for one year or more in Japan. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf)

Within the resident category, a non-Japanese individual who has had a domicile or residence in Japan for an aggregate period of five years or less within the preceding ten years is classified as a non-permanent resident. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf)

An individual other than a resident is a non-resident. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf)

The NTA guide warns that if a person who owns a residence in Japan leaves Japan temporarily and later re-enters Japan, the person may be treated as having resided in Japan during the absence, with facts such as family remaining in Japan, a retained home or hotel room, or daily-use property kept in Japan relevant to the temporary-absence presumption. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf)

The NTA's withholding materials state that resident/non-resident definitions are unrelated to nationality or residence status and are determined by facts such as whether the person has a domicile in Japan or has had a residence continuously for one year or more. [NTA Withholding Tax Guide PDF](https://www.nta.go.jp/publication/pamph/gensen/shikata_r05/pdf/16.pdf)

Practical caveat: immigration permission, a hotel booking, apartment lease, employer policy, and treaty residence certificate are evidence but do not alone settle Japanese tax residence; the tax question is fact-specific under the NTA resident/non-resident definitions and any applicable treaty. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf), [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm)

## Scope of Taxable Income

Residents other than non-permanent residents are subject to Japanese income tax on their whole income. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf)

Non-permanent residents are subject to Japanese income tax on income other than foreign-source income, foreign-source income paid in Japan, and foreign-source income paid abroad and remitted to Japan. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf)

For non-permanent residents, the NTA explains a deemed-remittance ordering rule: remittances received in Japan from abroad are first treated as remittances of income other than foreign-source income paid abroad, and only the excess is treated as remitted foreign-source income. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf)

Non-residents are subject to Japanese income tax on domestic-source income and are not taxed on income other than domestic-source income. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf)

NTA's domestic-source income explanation states that non-residents and foreign corporations are taxed only on domestic-source income, while residents are generally taxed on income earned inside and outside Japan. [NTA No.2878 domestic-source income](https://www.nta.go.jp/taxes/shiraberu/taxanswer/gensen/2878.htm)

## Japan-Source Employment and Business/Service Income for Remote Work

Japan-source work income turns heavily on where the work or personal service is physically performed: NTA materials list salaries and other rewards for provision of personal services among domestic-source income for non-residents, and NTA's domestic-source income page lists salaries, wages, and remuneration for services connected with work or personal services carried out in Japan. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf), [NTA No.2878 domestic-source income](https://www.nta.go.jp/taxes/shiraberu/taxanswer/gensen/2878.htm)

The Income Tax Act framework, reflected in official translations, treats certain salaries, remuneration, and pensions arising from employment or personal services in Japan as domestic-source income. [Income Tax Act translation, Japanese Law Translation](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/52/en), [Order for Enforcement of the Income Tax Act translation](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3121/en)

For an employee of a foreign company who works online while physically in Japan, the Japan-source question cannot be answered only by asking where the employer is incorporated, where payroll is run, or where the bank account is located; workdays or service days in Japan may create domestic-source employment/personal-service income under the NTA framework. [NTA No.2878 domestic-source income](https://www.nta.go.jp/taxes/shiraberu/taxanswer/gensen/2878.htm), [NTA No.12021](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12021.htm)

For a freelancer or independent contractor, the Japan tax analysis may involve whether the income is business income, miscellaneous income, or compensation for personal services, and whether any treaty business-profits, independent-personal-services, PE, or fixed-base article changes Japan's taxing right. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide, income types](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/05.pdf), [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm), [MOF tax convention list](https://www.mof.go.jp/english/policy/tax_policy/tax_conventions/tax_convetion_list_en.html)

Practical caveat: "foreign-source income" is not the same as "paid by a foreign company" or "paid into a foreign account"; for remote work, Japan-source status may follow the location where employment or personal services are performed, subject to treaty relief where applicable. [NTA No.2878 domestic-source income](https://www.nta.go.jp/taxes/shiraberu/taxanswer/gensen/2878.htm), [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm)

## 183-Day and Tax Treaty Concepts

Japan's domestic income-tax residence rule uses "JUSHO (domicile)" and continuous "KYOSHO (residence)" for one year or more; the NTA residence definition is not a simple 183-day domestic test. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf)

NTA guidance for people with multiple countries of stay says a Japanese domicile means the person's base of life and is judged by objective facts such as housing, occupation, asset location, family residence, and nationality; the same guidance explicitly warns that residence is not determined only by stay days and that a person can still be a Japanese resident even after spending 183 days or more abroad if their base of life remains in Japan. [NTA No.2012](https://www.nta.go.jp/taxes/shiraberu/taxanswer/shotoku/2012.htm)

The NTA says Japan and the individual's country of residence may have a tax treaty to avoid double taxation and that treaty provisions differ depending on each treaty. [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm)

The NTA gives as an example that a non-resident may need to consider a tax treaty if they stay in Japan for 183 days or less and have earned income not paid by an employer in Japan and not borne by an employer's PE in Japan. [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm)

The Ministry of Finance maintains the official list of Japan's tax conventions and links to treaty texts, outlines, protocols, exchanged notes, and MLI synthesised texts where available. [MOF list of Japan's tax conventions](https://www.mof.go.jp/english/policy/tax_policy/tax_conventions/tax_convetion_list_en.html), [MOF MLI page](https://www.mof.go.jp/english/policy/tax_policy/tax_conventions/mli.htm)

Treaty caveat: "183 days" may be counted differently by treaty, for example by calendar year, taxable year, or any 12-month period, and employment-income relief usually also depends on employer, payment, and PE-borne-cost conditions; the exact treaty text must be checked. [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm), [MOF list of Japan's tax conventions](https://www.mof.go.jp/english/policy/tax_policy/tax_conventions/tax_convetion_list_en.html)

Digital Nomad caveat: the Digital Nomad status permits a stay of six months with no extension, but that immigration period is not identical to a tax treaty's 183-day count and does not by itself prove treaty residence in another country. [MOFA Digital Nomad visa page](https://www.mofa.go.jp/ca/fna/pagewe_000001_00046.html), [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm)

## Withholding and Final Return Basics

Japan's individual income tax uses a self-assessment system in which taxpayers calculate taxable income and tax and file a final return by the due date for income earned from January 1 to December 31, and Japan also uses withholding at source for specific income such as salaries, wages, retirement allowances, interest, dividends, and fees. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf)

NTA's 2025 guide describes the final return as the process for settling excess or deficiency against withheld tax or estimated prepayments for the calendar-year income period. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf)

For non-residents without a PE, NTA's non-resident tax page states that many categories of domestic-source income are subject to separate withholding taxation at source, generally at 20.42% for listed income categories and 15.315% for some interest-related categories. [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm)

For salaries, wages, other remuneration, or retirement allowances paid outside Japan and not subject to Japanese withholding, the NTA states that a non-resident must file a quasi-final tax return and pay the tax. [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm)

Before departure, residents and non-residents with income subject to aggregate taxation who leave Japan without designating a tax agent must file and pay before departure, while those who designate a tax agent can file by the statutory due date through the tax agent. [NTA No.12021](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12021.htm)

If an individual leaves Japan and loses domicile or residence for tax purposes but still has national tax procedures, they need to appoint a Japan-resident tax agent and submit a Notification of Tax Agent; if they leave without doing so and must file for the departure year, they must file a quasi-final return and pay before departure. [NTA No.12004](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12004.htm)

To claim treaty relief for income subject to Japanese withholding, NTA says the taxpayer needs to submit an Application Form for Income Tax Convention with attachments through the payer before payment; if the forms are late, NTA describes a refund route using the refund application through the payer. [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm), [NTA treaty application forms](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/withholing/tax_convention.htm)

## Local Inhabitant Tax Timing

Individual inhabitant tax is a local tax, and Tokyo's official tax guide explains that inhabitant tax on individuals consists of a per-income levy based on previous-year income and a per-capita levy. [Tokyo Metropolitan Tax Guide 2024](https://www.tax.metro.tokyo.lg.jp/book/guidebookgaigo/guidebook2024e.pdf)

Tokyo's official tax guide states that residents living in Tokyo as of January 1 are subject to the per-income and per-capita levies, which are imposed and collected by municipalities together with metropolitan, ward, and municipal inhabitant taxes. [Tokyo Metropolitan Tax Guide 2024](https://www.tax.metro.tokyo.lg.jp/book/guidebookgaigo/guidebook2024e.pdf)

Tokyo's guide says salaried workers pay inhabitant tax by special collection from salary from June through May of the following year, while individuals other than salaried workers generally receive a tax notice and pay in installments. [Tokyo Metropolitan Tax Guide 2024](https://www.tax.metro.tokyo.lg.jp/book/guidebookgaigo/guidebook2024e.pdf)

Tokyo's guide says previous-year income from January 1 to December 31 is declared by March 15 at the local municipality where the person resides as of January 1, but people who filed a final income tax return are generally treated as having filed the inhabitant tax return. [Tokyo Metropolitan Tax Guide 2022](https://www.tax.metro.tokyo.lg.jp/book/guidebookgaigo/guidebook2022e.pdf), [NTA inhabitant tax guide excerpt](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2020/16.pdf)

Practical caveat: short-stay digital nomads should not assume income tax and inhabitant tax use identical timing; inhabitant tax turns on January 1 local-residence rules and municipality procedures, so the relevant city/ward office should be checked if the person is resident-registered or receives a notice. [Tokyo Metropolitan Tax Guide 2024](https://www.tax.metro.tokyo.lg.jp/book/guidebookgaigo/guidebook2024e.pdf), [NTA inhabitant tax guide excerpt](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2020/16.pdf)

## Consumption Tax: Only a Flag for Business Operators

NTA's consumption tax basics state that the taxable period for sole proprietors is, in principle, January 1 through December 31. [NTA Consumption Tax Basic Knowledge](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/consumption_tax/01.htm)

NTA's consumption tax basics state that the base period for sole proprietors is, in principle, the second preceding year before the taxable period, and the base period is used for determining whether a business is a taxable person. [NTA Consumption Tax Basic Knowledge](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/consumption_tax/01.htm)

NTA's consumption tax basics define a foreign business by reference to non-residents under the Income Tax Act and foreign corporations under the Corporation Tax Act. [NTA Consumption Tax Basic Knowledge](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/consumption_tax/01.htm)

Practical caveat: consumption tax is usually not the core issue for a salaried employee, but it can matter for freelancers, sole proprietors, creators, consultants, or platform businesses if they make taxable business transactions, have Japan-side obligations, or choose/need invoice-system registration. [NTA Consumption Tax Basic Knowledge](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/consumption_tax/01.htm)

## Social Insurance, Pension, and Tax Residence Caveats

Japan's public pension system is not framed as an income-tax residence test: Japan Pension Service states that National Pension covers all residents of Japan between age 20 and 59, regardless of nationality, and Employees' Pension Insurance covers company and public-sector workers. [Japan Pension Service English overview](https://www.nenkin.go.jp/international/english/index.html), [Japan Pension Service enrollment page](https://www.nenkin.go.jp/international/english/purpose/enroll/index.html)

Japan Pension Service's English National Pension pamphlet states that all people registered to reside in Japan and aged between 20 and 59, regardless of nationality, must be covered by the National Pension system and pay contributions by law. [Japan Pension Service National Pension pamphlet](https://www.nenkin.go.jp/service/pamphlet/kaigai/kokunenseido.files/2English.pdf)

MHLW materials explain that, excluding short-term visas and similar cases, foreign nationals with proper residence status and an address in Japan are covered by Japan's medical insurance system and pay premiums while receiving benefits. [MHLW foreign residents medical insurance material](https://www.mhlw.go.jp/content/12401000/001616165.pdf)

MHLW guidance for foreign workers states that National Health Insurance benefits are provided to those residing more than three months, such as medium-to-long-term residents, special permanent residents, and certain protected or long-term visitors. [MHLW foreign nationals work guidance PDF](https://www.mhlw.go.jp/file/06-Seisakujouhou-11650000-Shokugyouanteikyokuhakenyukiroudoutaisakubu/0000055573.pdf)

Digital Nomad-specific caveat: MOFA requires private insurance for Digital Nomad applicants, and ISA materials state that Digital Nomad status is outside residence-card issuance; therefore a Digital Nomad's public health insurance and pension position should be checked separately from income-tax residence and with the local office or competent agency. [MOFA Digital Nomad visa page](https://www.mofa.go.jp/ca/fna/pagewe_000001_00046.html), [ISA Digital Nomad outline PDF](https://www.moj.go.jp/isa/content/001417262.pdf), [Japan Pension Service National Pension pamphlet](https://www.nenkin.go.jp/service/pamphlet/kaigai/kokunenseido.files/2English.pdf)

## Suggested Article Caveats

Do not write "Digital Nomad visa holders never owe Japanese tax"; Japanese tax can apply to domestic-source income, and Japan-source work income can arise from work performed physically in Japan. [NTA 2025 Income Tax Guide](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/pdf/incometax_2025/04.pdf), [NTA No.2878 domestic-source income](https://www.nta.go.jp/taxes/shiraberu/taxanswer/gensen/2878.htm)

Do not write "under 183 days means no Japan tax"; the NTA frames 183 days as one treaty fact pattern, and treaty relief also depends on employer, PE, payer/procedure, treaty residence, and the exact treaty text. [NTA No.12006](https://www.nta.go.jp/english/taxes/individual/12006.htm), [MOF list of Japan's tax conventions](https://www.mof.go.jp/english/policy/tax_policy/tax_conventions/tax_convetion_list_en.html)

Do not write "foreign employer means foreign-source income"; NTA domestic-source rules focus on work or personal services carried out in Japan for salary/remuneration source analysis. [NTA No.2878 domestic-source income](https://www.nta.go.jp/taxes/shiraberu/taxanswer/gensen/2878.htm), [Income Tax Act translation, Japanese Law Translation](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/52/en)

Do not imply that local inhabitant tax follows the same timing as national income tax; inhabitant tax is generally based on previous-year income and January 1 municipal residence. [Tokyo Metropolitan Tax Guide 2024](https://www.tax.metro.tokyo.lg.jp/book/guidebookgaigo/guidebook2024e.pdf)

Do not state that social insurance and pension follow income-tax residence; pension and medical insurance materials use registered residence/address/employment concepts, while Digital Nomad immigration materials require private insurance and state the status is outside residence-card issuance. [Japan Pension Service National Pension pamphlet](https://www.nenkin.go.jp/service/pamphlet/kaigai/kokunenseido.files/2English.pdf), [MHLW foreign residents medical insurance material](https://www.mhlw.go.jp/content/12401000/001616165.pdf), [MOFA Digital Nomad visa page](https://www.mofa.go.jp/ca/fna/pagewe_000001_00046.html), [ISA Digital Nomad outline PDF](https://www.moj.go.jp/isa/content/001417262.pdf)
